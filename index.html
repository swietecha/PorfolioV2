Quarter: Summer 2014

Skills:

Tonique

Design | Branding

Objective

Your team has been selected to design the headquarters for Tonique, a company that specializes in men's body products. The concept of their brand/space is based on their signature fragrance. Collaborate with Interior Design students to create and brand a signature high-end male fragrance retail store and business area. The interior design student designed the store. I designed the logo, branding, packaging, and website.

Class: Media Design

Quarter: Spring 2016

Skills:

  • Collaboration
  • Branding
  • Packaging Design
  • Web Design
View Style Guide View Prototype Site

Challenge

Before this project, I collaborated with other students, but they were either Graphic Designers or Web Designers. This was the first time working with an Interior Design student. Their learning is more of a physical process and their terminology is entirely different. We actually got along very well with each other and this made it easier for us to explain to each other what we knew and our process. I had never put together a style guide before. We were given examples and from those, I was able to pull together all my assets to make a style guide that I feel proud about.

Mechanical Pencil Training

Design | Development | UI/UX

Objective

Build a website to showcase how to care for a mechanical pencil. In this simple website you will need to showcase three different learning styles and one quiz to show that the user understands what you have tried to teach them about the care of the mechanical pencil.

Class: E-Learning Design and Applications

Quarter: Spring 2016

Skills:

  • Research
  • Paper Prototype
  • Testing
  • HTML
  • CSS
  • PHP
  • Javascript
  • jQuery
  • Responsive Design
  • User Experience
View Documentation View Live Site

Challenge

The topic of this project was the main stumbling block. Everyone knows how a mechanical pencil works. So I had to research everything there is to know about a mechanical pencil in order to construct proper training. I also struggled with putting together a time line and project plan. This required me to analyze what I had to do and when I was going to do it. The process of not being able to start something without finishing something else first was eye opening. I eventually was able to create a logical process from start to finish.

Restroom Finder App

UI/UX

Objective

Create an MVP (Minimum Viable Product) product while working in an agile environment.

Class: User Experience

Quarter: Spring 2017

Skills:

  • Research
  • Wireframes
  • Paper Prototype
  • Clickable Prototype
  • Testing
View Documentation View Clickable Site

Challenge

There were several challenges for this project. First, I am not in the habit of putting together a minimal viable product. I wanted to take the design further than I needed to. I kept myself in check by reminding myself, this didn't have to be perfect. The next challenge was writing the questions to get an in depth answer. I had to go through several iterations before I was able to have the proper questions. The last challenge was to verbally convey the logistics of what happens when a user presses a button. With diagrams and white board, I was able to visually see the path of each action.
